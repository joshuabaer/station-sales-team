Where We Are Today

Seventeen years. 1,083 portfolio companies. Three cities. Josh personally backstopped ~$20M through COVID to keep this thing alive.

Corporates want to work with startups. They genuinely do. They just don't know how — they sponsor a summit, put their logo on a stage, write a $50K check to feel innovative, and nothing happens. The startup gets a business card. The corporate gets a slide deck. Nobody closes a deal.

Government is the other half of the same problem. The technology exists. The mission need exists. The procurement system makes it nearly impossible to connect them. FAR was designed for Lockheed, not for a 12-person robotics company that could solve the problem in 90 days.

That's the market failure we've spent 17 years trying to fix.

In August 2024, DARPA awarded us a $25M Broad Pricing Agreement — HR001124A0002. No other accelerator has one. That's the moat.

Where We're Going

Capital Factory is a for-profit venture firm. STATION Earth is a 501(c)(3) civic anchor. Same building, shared mission, separate books — always.

This is not a pivot. Not a real estate play. Not a rebrand.

DARPA didn't pick us because we're a coworking company. They picked us because of 17 years and 1,083 equity-aligned portfolio companies. Allied governments want that access. Defense primes want it. The machine is already built.

What's already in the bank: SXSW 2026 closed $494K. Baker Botts is Year 7 of 9. The DARPA BPA is live and generating $185K per quarter. And the portfolio keeps producing — Apptronik raised $350M+ in humanoid robotics, Saronic is building autonomous warships, ICON is printing houses and moon bases with NASA contracts, Firefly just landed on the moon, Paradromics is wiring neural interfaces for DARPA. Field AI raised $400M. That's the proof the machine works. That's the base we're building from.

Seven bets have to land in the next 24 months. If they do, we go from $12M to $43M. Here's what they are.
